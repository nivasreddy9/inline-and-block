Rohit Sharma

Early Life and Career

+

Full Name: Rohit Gurunath Sharma

Date of Birth: April 30, 1987

Birthplace: Nagpur, Maharashtra, India

Role: Batsman, occasional bowler

Batting Style: Right-handed

Bowling Style: Right-arm off-break

Domestic Career

Rohit Sharma made his debut in domestic cricket for Mumbai in the 2006-07 season. His consistent performances in domestic cricket earned him a place in the Indian national team.

International Career

ODI Debut: June 23, 2007, against Ireland

T20I Debut: September 19, 2007, against England

Test Debut: November 6, 2013, against West Indies

Achievements

  • Only cricketer to score three double centuries in One Day Internationals (ODIs).
  • Highest individual score in an ODI innings (264 runs against Sri Lanka in 2014).
  • Most T20 International centuries (4).
  • Fastest century in T20 Internationals (off 35 balls).

Captaincy

Successful captain of the Mumbai Indians (MI) in the Indian Premier League (IPL), leading the team to five titles (2013, 2015, 2017, 2019, and 2020). Under his leadership, India has won several bilateral series and performed well in multi-nation tournaments.

Awards

  • Arjuna Award (2015)
  • Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna Award, India's highest sporting honor (2020)
  • ICC ODI Cricketer of the Year (2019)

Personal Life

Family: Rohit Sharma is married to Ritika Sajdeh, and the couple has a daughter named Samaira.

Playing Style

Rohit Sharma is known for his elegant and effortless batting technique. He has a wide range of shots and is particularly strong on the pull shot. His ability to pace his innings and accelerate at will makes him one of the most dangerous batsmen in limited-overs cricket.

Contributions to Indian Cricket

Rohit Sharma has been instrumental in several of India's crucial victories over the years. His experience and calm demeanor make him a vital player and leader for the team. His contributions extend beyond the field, as he is also involved in various philanthropic activities and campaigns for wildlife conservation.
